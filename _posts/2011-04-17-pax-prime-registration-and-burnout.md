This weekend, my wife and I registered for <a title="PAX Website" href="http://prime.paxsite.com/" target="_blank">PAX Prime</a>. For those of you who don&#8217;t know, PAX is the Penny Arcade Expo and is a gaming convention that covers the spectrum: console, tabletop, boardgames, and pen and paper. In addition to all the games, several panels will be held and there&#8217;s a good chance some of my favorite geek celebrities will be in attendance. In addition to the amazing convention, I&#8217;m looking forward to taking my wife around Seattle and showing her all the cool sites I saw when I was at <a title="Finding Myself at An Event Apart 2011 (Seattle, WA)" href="http://www.levihackwith.com/finding-myself-at-an-event-apart-2011-seattle-wa/" target="_blank">An Event Apart</a> back in March.

<!--more-->

Right after we registered for PAX, I realized that this would be my first real vacation in over a year. In fact, it&#8217;s been so long since I&#8217;ve taken any PTO at work, that I&#8217;ve had to start taking random days off to keep from &#8220;maxing out&#8221; my vacation time. That being said, I&#8217;ve found that I&#8217;m suffering from a serious case of burnout.

Off the clock and on the weekends, I&#8217;ve always done my best to try and create something that is both educational for myself and beneficial to the development community at large. Unfortunately, with all the stress and pressures that have been occurring with my other commitments, I&#8217;ve been unable to sit down and produce anything genuinely useful in quite some time. I&#8217;ve discovered that doing something for 8 hours out of every day &#8211; no matter how much you love it &#8211; makes it really hard to do that same thing once the major incentives are removed (mainly not being a homeless bum). My goal in the coming days is to find a motivation that doesn&#8217;t involve the threat of homelessness as well as take a few days at PAX to push all my current stresses, deadlines, and commitments to the side and _freaking relax!_

My hope with beginning to blog again was to create a place where I could make a positive impact on the Internet and not just contribute to the mindless drivel all over the Internet. While that is still my goal, I&#8217;ve come to realize that, in addition to being a resource, this blog needs to function as a place for me to articulate and share the sometimes random thoughts and feelings that bounce around inside my head.  I hope that if you can&#8217;t find something education or informative, you at least find a kindred spirit.
