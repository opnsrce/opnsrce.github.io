Two big thing occurred this week: I got a Kindle and I got glasses (the reading / driving kind). Now, I already own an iPad, so why get a Kindle? Well, while the iPad supports e-books, it does so as more of an afterthought than a main feature. Due to it&#8217;s small size, light weight, and use of Electronic Paper, the Kindle is much more suited for those situations where space is at a premium. Don&#8217;t let it&#8217;s small footprint fool you, however. The Kindle is capable of storing hundreds of e-books, giving you the ability to bring your entire reading library wherever you have time to kill. If you&#8217;d like to learn more about the advantages of owning a Kindle, you can check out my wife&#8217;s post &#8220;<a href="http://amandahackwith.com/tech/why-i-read-more-on-my-kindle/" target="_blank">Why I Read More on My Kindle</a>.&#8221;

## New Specs

Over the past couple of months, I&#8217;ve found myself having difficulty reading street signs when driving, especially at night. Given the amount of time I spent in front of a computer screen, I figured it was time to get my eyes checked. After seeing the eye doc, I was diagnosed with an astigmatism in my left eye that would need correction with eyeglasses.

Picking out eyeglasses is no easy task, especially when your pupils are dilated to the the size of nickels. Luckily, I brought my wife to offer a second opinion on any selection I might choose. And the results? Well, you tell me.
